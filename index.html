Overview

Modern Bioeconomy produces energy and many forms of materials for value added products. The main raw material is round wood harvested from Boreal zone forests. Timber harvesting and transport logistics in boreal forests, such as those found in Finland, are highly dependent on the mobility of forest machinery and timber trucks on forest roads and in terrain. Mobility of forest machinery as well as rut formation, is highly dependent on soil bearing capacity.

The Harvester Seasons service is providing dynamic soil bearing capacity nowcasting and forecasting while coupling this information with the relatively more static factors to produce tangible information products and services for both vehicle operators as well as for logistics planners alike.

Background

A thorough understanding of soil thermal and moisture regimes is critical for a wide variety of disciplines and engineering applications, as is the case with the proposed service. Soil thermal and moisture regimes exhibit strong variability in both space and time. In situ measurements of soil temperature and moisture are available, but currently such information is not available at spatial scales required for supporting the operational needs of timber collection and transport logistics in bor eal forests. In order to move from point scale in-situ measurements to spatially distributed information at relevant thematic accuracies, modelling needs to be used.

The Finnish Meteorological Institute (FMI) has developed a semi-physical, gridded 5x5 km hydrological forecasting system (HOPS – Hydrological Operations and Prediction System), which is used to simulate land-surface fluxes and predict the behaviour of the land surface under different conditions. In combination with static aerial laser scanning analysis (ALS) data by Arbonaut Oy soil trafficability classifications are provided in 16m resolution for Finnish forest lands.

Service

The current service is offering meteorological information like precipitation and temperature as well as the soil trafficability based on the hydrological forecast. The top right of the map layer icon lets you choose between different variables to be visualized. Timeseries of all variables for selected locations (default is the city of Kajaani) can be achieved by clicking at any point on the map. Moving the cursor over the graph is returning exact values for the selected variable at each timestep. Dragging allows zooming the graphs, double click to zoom out.

The service is still under construction.
